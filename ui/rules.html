Lockpick Lab Rules

Deterministic, single-player puzzles. No randomness, no hidden information.

Core Objective

  • Reduce the boss health to 0 within the target number of rounds.
  • The boss does not take turns; it is a fixed puzzle state.
  • All information is visible: hands, boards, and effects.

Setup

  • Boss health and target rounds are set by the puzzle.
  • You start with a fixed amount of mana and a defined hand.
  • Mana per round is fixed by the puzzle and carries over.
  • Enemy creatures may already be on the board.

Zones

  • Player board: your creatures in play.
  • Enemy board: boss creatures in play.
  • Hand: cards available to play this turn.

Resources

  • Mana pays the cost of playing cards.
  • Mana carries over between rounds.
  • Power is the single combat stat. It is both damage and resilience.

Card Types

  • Creatures enter the board and can attack once per round.
  • Spells resolve instantly and then leave play.
  • Effects stay on the battlefield and modify rules.
  • Mods attach to a creature and modify its attributes.

Actions & Turn Flow

  • You may take any number of actions in any order until you end the round.
  • Creatures can attack once per round, then become tired.
  • At end of round, all tired creatures become rested.
  • Mana refills according to the puzzle setup.

Combat Rules

  • If the boss has any creatures on the board, you must attack a creature.
  • If there are no enemy creatures, you may attack the boss directly.
  • When a creature attacks another creature, both deal their power simultaneously.
  • Any creature reduced to 0 power is destroyed.

Keywords & Mechanics

Guard

While a Guard creature exists, you must attack a Guard first.

Storm

Can attack any target immediately on the turn it is played.

Pierce

Excess power from a creature attack spills over to the boss.

Chain

Triggers a bonus effect if you have already played a card this round.

Sacrifice

Destroy a friendly creature to trigger an effect.

Determinism

  • No randomness. No hidden information. Every puzzle is fully knowable.
  • Identical actions always result in identical outcomes.
